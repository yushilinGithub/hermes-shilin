# Index playbooks — CSI 300 / CSI 1000 / ChiNext-STAR / offshore China-tech

## CSI 300 (沪深300) — large-cap benchmark
- Top 300 names across Shanghai + Shenzhen, ~half of total A-share market cap. **Beta ≈ 1.0** —
  it *defines* "the market," lower daily vol than the small-cap / tech indices.
- **Most sensitive to:** policy, macro, foreign flows, and a few heavyweight stocks.
- **Sector blocs:** financials ~20–22% (banks, insurers, brokers) is the largest and swings
  the index most; consumer staples (liquor) is the next big bloc.
- **Heavyweight single stocks that move the index:** Kweichow Moutai (often the single largest
  weight, ~5–6%; its wholesale price 批价, sales, buybacks matter), plus CATL, BYD, Midea,
  China Merchants Bank, Ping An.
- **The national-team channel:** state intervention runs predominantly through CSI 300 ETFs
  (Huatai-PineBridge 510300 is the giant; also 159919, 510330). Tell: late-session ramp into
  the close on abnormally heavy ETF volume.
- **Bank nuance:** LPR cut compresses NIM short-term; "中特估 / high-dividend state-owned" rotation
  makes big state banks a defensive flight-to-safety trade *within* China.

## CSI 1000 (中证1000) — small cap
- **Highest beta** of the three — this is where the sharp -2% days cluster.
- **Most sensitive to:** liquidity, retail/quant flows, **snowball & DMA deleveraging**,
  national-team rotation, microcap sentiment. Least driven by fundamentals day-to-day.

## ChiNext / STAR ("onshore China Nasdaq") — growth/tech
- ChiNext (创业板) + STAR (科创板): semiconductors, batteries, biotech, new energy, AI/compute.
- **Most sensitive to:** US tech overnight (**SOX, Mag-7**), chip sanctions/export controls,
  AI themes, and rate/duration (long-duration growth). Falls hardest in a global tech derate.
- On a tech-led down day this drops far more than the CSI 300 (e.g. ChiNext -4% vs CSI 300 -1.8%).

## CQQQ / KWEB ("offshore China Nasdaq") — China internet ADRs / HK tech
- Alibaba, Tencent, Meituan, PDD, etc. **Trades on US hours** → driven by US–China relations,
  USD/CNY, the Fed, and megacap earnings. Different driver set from the onshore names, and a
  useful **overnight sentiment read** for the next A-share / HK-tech session.

## Quick cross-index intuition
- Tech-led selloff → ChiNext ≫ CSI 1000 > CSI 300 in magnitude.
- Policy/national-team support day → CSI 300 holds up best (state buys the large-cap ETFs).
- Liquidity squeeze / leverage cascade → CSI 1000 hit hardest.
- Use A50 futures + KWEB/CQQQ overnight as the leading tell before the 09:30 CST open.
