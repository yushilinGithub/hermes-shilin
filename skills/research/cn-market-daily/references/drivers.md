# Driver taxonomy — what moves Chinese equity indices on a single day

A single ±1–2% day is normal volatility and almost always the *sum* of several overlapping
forces, not one clean cause. Ranked by typical daily impact on A-shares.

## 1. Policy & regulatory (the dominant onshore swing factor)
More than in almost any other major market. Watch:
- Politburo / Central Economic Work Conference signals
- PBOC actions: RRR cut, LPR change, MLF, reverse-repo injections; money-market rate DR007
- "National team" (Central Huijin) buying — done **through broad ETFs**, esp. CSI 300 ETFs
- Property-easing measures, regulatory crackdown/easing
- The *tone* of state media (Securities Times / Xinhua front-page editorials)

## 2. Liquidity / monetary conditions
RRR/MLF/LPR moves and funding rates. Tight funding hits small caps hardest. National-team
buying shows up as heavy ETF creation in CSI 300 / 500 / 1000.

## 3. Macro data prints
Official + Caixin PMI, CPI/PPI (deflation anxiety), GDP, credit/total social financing,
exports, and especially property sales. Misses feed the "more stimulus needed" narrative.

## 4. External / global
Overnight US (Nasdaq, **SOX semis, Mag-7** — lead the tech/growth indices), the Fed, USD/CNY,
10Y UST yields, US–China headlines (tariffs, chip export controls, entity-list). MSCI/FTSE
rebalances. (Daily northbound Stock Connect flow data was hidden starting 2024.)

## 5. Flows & derivatives mechanics
Margin financing (融资融券) levels, index-futures basis (IF/IC/IM), options expiry, and
crucially **structured-product cascades** — e.g. snowball (雪球) knock-ins and quant/DMA
deleveraging drove the Feb-2024 small-cap crash. These make selloffs sharp.

## 6. Retail sentiment / herding
A-share turnover is ~60%+ retail, so momentum amplifies; turnover spikes are a tell.

## The +1% / -2% asymmetry
Chinese selloffs tend to be sharper than rallies. Downside is fed by leverage/derivative
cascades and fragile sentiment (forced unwinds compound); upside is usually a policy-driven
grind unless there's a genuine stimulus surprise. So a -2% day is more often a flow/
deleveraging event; a +1% day is more often a policy/liquidity-headline event.

## Daily read (apply in order)
1. Overnight global — US close, USD/CNY, any US–China headline.
2. Policy/data — anything from PBOC, Politburo, or a major print (PMI/TSF/property) that morning.
3. Who led it — financials + Moutai together = macro/policy/national-team day; a narrow tech
   or new-energy move = thematic day; small-cap-led flush = flow/leverage story.
4. Close behavior + ETF volume — a late ramp on heavy CSI 300 ETF volume suggests state buying.
